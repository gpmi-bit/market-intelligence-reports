Battery Brand Global Weekly Report

Executive Summary

The global battery sector is currently marked by a sharp divergence between Western legacy brands and Asian manufacturers, with Energizer undergoing manufacturing retrenchment in the Americas while China’s Nanfu reports record profits and massive capacity expansion. A significant strategic rift has emerged regarding chemistry: Panasonic has explicitly rejected LFP technology for high-value segments, instead positioning ternary cells as the future standard for humanoid robotics. Concurrently, major players are aggressively diversifying into residential energy storage (ESS) and specialized EV auxiliary markets to offset volatility in the traditional alkaline retail segment.

Key Intelligence

Operational Shifts & Manufacturing

  • Energizer Holdings (NYSE: ENR) is fully idling its manufacturing facility in Marietta, Ohio, resulting in the layoff of its entire workforce. The closure is attributed to facility disrepair and lack of long-term investment, reflecting broader operational restructuring as the company's stock trades near 52-week lows. Read Source
  • Anfu Technology (Nanfu Battery) reported a 34.38% increase in 2025 net profit, with Nanfu maintaining its #1 market share in China for 33 consecutive years. The company announced plans to add 500 million units of new battery production capacity in 2026. Read Source
  • Panasonic Energy has completed the automation of its alkaline production at the new Nishikinohama flagship factory in Japan. The facility uses advanced monorail and automated storage systems to maintain cost competitiveness against a global alkaline market valued at $8.9 billion. Read Source

Strategic Chemistry & Robotics

  • Panasonic Energy CTO Shoichiro Watanabe stated at InterBattery 2026 that the company will not produce LFP batteries, even for the US energy storage market. Panasonic is instead betting on ternary (NCM/NCA) cylindrical cells for the emerging humanoid robotics sector, citing a need for high energy density that LFP cannot meet. Read Source
  • Energizer, via licensing partner Avenir Telecom, is launching the P30K Apex smartphone featuring a 30,000mAh battery. While designed for extreme endurance, Chinese media highlights that the capacity exceeds civil aviation carry-on limits, potentially limiting its practical adoption in Asian markets. Read Source

Diversification: ESS & EV Aftermarket

  • Varta has launched the Vartawall BM2, a modular LFP-based home storage system supporting bidirectional charging (V2H). The system is designed for extreme outdoor temperatures and marks Varta’s push into the European residential solar-plus-storage market. Read Source
  • Duracell Energy has established a manufacturing partnership with UK-based Puredrive Energy to supply home and commercial energy storage systems across Europe and Africa, targeting local government and housing association contracts. Read Source
  • Varta has successfully positioned its A10 battery as the designated 12V auxiliary replacement for the Ford Mustang Mach-E, signaling a strategic focus on the specialized EV auxiliary battery replacement market in Europe. Read Source

Supply Chain & M&A

  • Panasonic Energy signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Frontier Lithium for long-term offtake of battery-grade lithium from Ontario. This move supports Panasonic's goal of 50% localized material procurement for its North American manufacturing plants. Read Source
  • Duracell’s US distribution network has been impacted by ORS Nasco’s acquisition of Alliance Distribution Partners. The deal integrates Duracell’s retail supply segment into a larger industrial MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations) wholesaler platform. Read Source

Action Items

  • Monitor Competitive Capacity Expansion: Evaluate the impact of Nanfu’s 500-million-unit capacity increase on regional pricing power. Assess if GP Batteries can maintain shelf-share in the China/HK markets as Nanfu accelerates its "second growth curve" and international OEM exports.
  • Evaluate EV Auxiliary Market Entry: Following Varta’s successful OEM-adjacent positioning for EV auxiliary 12V batteries, management should audit the current product portfolio for compatibility with leading EV models in the Asia-Pacific region to capture high-margin replacement demand.
  • Review Manufacturing Resilience: In light of Energizer’s Ohio plant closure due to facility "disrepair," initiate a high-level audit of existing manufacturing assets to ensure capital expenditure is sufficient to prevent sudden operational failures or forced shutdowns that could jeopardize supply continuity.
